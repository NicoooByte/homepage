My skills include C# in combination with the game engine Unity or .NET, Python, JavaScript, Typescript, CSS, HTML and MySQL. Versioning systems like Git or SourceTree are also familiar to me.

In 2022 I graduated the HTBLUvA Salzburg with excellence (Advanced Diploma in Multimedia Arts). There, apart from my IT skills, I have been taught a skillset in the fields of human-centered design, project planning and time management.

From August 2021 until September 2022 I got my first job, where my two colleagues and I built an underground operations VR simulator for the Austrian Armed Forces. In addition, I contributed to an application with which tunnels, subway stations, etc. can be reconstructed as quickly as possible in a 3D environment. For further information visit the following links [1][2].

After that I fulfilled my compulsory social service at the Red Cross in Salzburg, which taught me how to work effectivly under stress. Following these rather excrutiating nine months I decided to go on a working holiday in Australia. While I was there I created and hosted a Website for Xeron Systems and created a small C# dynamic linked library which involved (legally) scrapping data.

Late February this year I started being self-employed as a Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) trainer specialising in coding problems. The odds are high that your favorite LLM was partially trained by guys like me.

Now, I am looking for a stable job where I don't have to manage my taxes and insurance (mostly because it is to expensive to be lucrative). Privately, I have realized several paid and unpaid projects ranging from simple web scrapers and web automations to Android reverse engineering, small ML models, a boids simulation and a port scanner. Right now, I am diversifying my knowledge by reading books, most recently "Antifragility".

I hope to have left a lasting impression and would like to hear back from you.
